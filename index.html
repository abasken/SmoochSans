Smooch Sans

Smooch Sans, designed by Robert E. Leuschke and published through Art & Sign Fonts (Leuschke Fonts), is a neo-grotesque Sans-serif typeface that has been updated with an extensive character set and designed as a Variable font to a offer nine weight variations of the clean sans font.
Leuschke worked at hallmark before creating his own design business. That hallmark neo-grotesque style is displayed in the layout of the typeface. Leuschke subtle influence can be seen in each individual lettering with their rounded shape.
Smooch Sans was originally designed as a truncated font to be used to compliment the Smooch brush script file, and is best used for modern, dynamic layouts due to its sleek rounded shape design. Its dual role as a functional sans and a stylistic bridge to Smooch script makes it a flexible tool when rely on a font for an expressive yet minimalistic typeface.
